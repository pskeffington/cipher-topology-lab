\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This project develops a reproducible framework for testing persistent homology as an auxiliary diagnostic for symmetric-cipher output randomness and generated byte-stream controls. The completed internal evidence now includes a six-condition 64-replicate run, an eight-condition weak-control sensitivity extension, and an alternate-seed robustness check.

Across these registered runs, the strongest and most reproducible positive-control evidence occurs for deliberately structured weak controls. LCG separates from the SHA-256 seeded baseline in the primary six-condition run, and deliberately periodic and biased byte-stream controls separate strongly in the weak-control extension and alternate-seed robustness check. AES-CTR deterministic-output conditions, OS CSPRNG output, and xorshift32 do not show comparable top-ranked separation under the current embeddings and feature set.

The thesis contribution is therefore methodological and diagnostic. It shows how to build an auditable pipeline that generates controlled streams, embeds them as point-cloud representations, computes persistent-homology features, validates artifact coherence and backend status, summarizes effect sizes, and registers evidence for claim traceability. The study is deliberately scoped as computational diagnostics rather than cryptanalytic attack development. Its value rests on transparent data generation, careful controls, reproducible code, evidence-register traceability, and restrained interpretation.

Future work should complete external randomness testing in an environment where Dieharder or a comparable statistical battery is available, add additional embeddings and configured cubical-complex experiments, and only then consider broader comparisons across additional cipher families or legacy comparators. Until such evidence is registered, the appropriate claim remains narrow: persistent-homology summaries can reproducibly distinguish deliberately structured weak-control streams under the tested design, but they do not certify security or establish a cipher vulnerability.
