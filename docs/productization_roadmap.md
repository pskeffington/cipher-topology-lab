# Productization Roadmap

## Positioning

`cipher-topology-lab` should be positioned as a reproducible diagnostic and evidence-control platform for generated byte-stream analysis. The productizable value is not a claim that the platform breaks ciphers. The value is the disciplined workflow: controlled data generation, topological summaries, baseline comparison, conventional diagnostic comparison, validation logs, evidence registers, claim boundaries, and manuscript/report generation.

## Market-facing lanes

### 1. AI/security methods lane

Primary package:

> A reproducible diagnostic workflow for evaluating structure in generated byte streams and documenting evidence boundaries.

Potential users:

- AI security teams;
- cybersecurity research groups;
- technical due-diligence reviewers;
- model-evaluation and red-team programs;
- research labs that need reproducible diagnostic workflows.

What to show:

- deterministic demo run;
- weak-control separation report;
- evidence-register traceability;
- explicit claim boundary.

What not to claim:

- cipher break;
- vulnerability discovery;
- production security certification;
- proof of randomness.

### 2. Reproducible research engineering lane

Primary package:

Potential users:

- research operations groups;

What to show:

### 3. Evidence-register platform lane

## Product tiers

### Tier A — Demo package

Purpose: let a reviewer understand the platform quickly.

Deliverables:

- `make platform-demo`;
- `results/platform/platform_demo_report.md`;
- `docs/platform_walkthrough.md`;
- small deterministic artifact set.

Estimated readiness: near-term.

### Tier B — Sponsored research package

Purpose: apply the diagnostic workflow to a bounded generated-data or weak-control question.

Deliverables:

- scoped diagnostic protocol;
- controlled run configuration;
- generated artifacts;
- evidence register;
- final technical report;
- limitations and claim-boundary section.

Estimated readiness: after platform-report generator and walkthrough are complete.

### Tier C — Evidence-register engine

Purpose: generalize the evidence system beyond this repo.

Deliverables:

- platform object schema;
- reusable evidence-register template;
- artifact inventory format;
- claim-boundary validator;
- CV valuation ingestion path.

Estimated readiness: after platform object export is stable.

### Tier D — Manuscript/thesis package

Purpose: preserve the academic path.

Deliverables:

- completed expansion ladder;
- manuscript tables and figures;
- thesis-ready discussion and limitations;
- optional external randomness comparison if completed.

Estimated readiness: after expansion execution and manuscript compile.

## Pricing and valuation posture

Use pricing only as planning language until buyer discovery is complete.

Recommended planning posture:

- Demo package: internal portfolio asset; no external price until walkthrough exists.
- Sponsored research package: price as a bounded technical report or grant-backed pilot.
- Evidence-register engine: price as a reusable methodology/platform component.
- Manuscript/thesis package: value as credibility and evidence base, not standalone SaaS.

## Conversion gates

A repo should not move from platform candidate to product candidate until these gates are satisfied:

1. A reviewer can run or inspect a deterministic demo.
2. A non-specialist can understand the walkthrough.
3. Claims are bounded and validated.
4. Evidence artifacts are discoverable.
5. A buyer/sponsor use case is named.
6. The next action is tied to either a pilot, paper, or platform module.
